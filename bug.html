This is an example of an uncommon HTML bug that involves incorrect nesting of elements within a form.

<form> <label for="name">Name:</label> <input type="text" id="name" name="name"> <fieldset> <legend>Address</legend> <label for="street">Street:</label> <input type="text" id="street" name="street"> <label for="city">City:</label> <input type="text" id="city" name="city"> </fieldset> <button type="submit">Submit</button> </form>

The issue is that the <fieldset> and <legend> elements are nested within the <form> element but the submit button is outside the fieldset. While this might render visually fine in most browsers, it can lead to unexpected behavior in terms of form submission or accessibility. Depending on the browser and its handling of form elements, the 'Submit' button might not be properly associated with the form's data, causing submission to fail or the data to not be captured correctly.
